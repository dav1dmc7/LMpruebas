Services

At López Márquez Abogados, we specialize in offering personalized and effective legal solutions. Our team is committed to professional excellence and the satisfaction of our clients, providing advice and representation in various areas of law.

    • Administrative Law

    We assist with appeals and claims against the Public Administration, including sanctioning procedures, tenders, public contracts, expropriations, and urban planning issues. We guide you in your interactions with governmental entities to ensure the best possible outcome.

    • Civil Law

    We support you in matters related to civil contracts, inheritances and successions, property and real rights, as well as debt collection and civil liability claims. We provide comprehensive advice to protect your personal and financial interests.

    • Immigration Law

    We offer assistance with residence and work permits, nationality applications, family reunification, and appeals against refusals. Our goal is to facilitate the immigration process and ensure compliance with the current regulations.

    • Labor Law

    We advise on labor disputes, the drafting and review of contracts, dismissals, salary claims, collective bargaining, and compliance with regulations. We work to protect your labor rights and offer solutions tailored to your needs.

    • Criminal Law

    We provide defense in criminal proceedings, representing our clients in economic, corporate, and private crimes. We are committed to protecting your fundamental rights at every stage of the judicial process.

We invite you to explore more about each of our services in the dedicated sections of our website. There, you'll find detailed information and additional resources that may interest you.

Contact us for a personalized consultation and discover how we can help you. We are committed to providing close and accessible service.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a labor process take?

The duration of a labor process can vary depending on the complexity of the case and the court's workload, but generally, it can range from 6 months to a year.

What documents do I need to start an immigration procedure?

Documents vary depending on the procedure, but typically, you'll need a passport, a criminal background certificate, and documentation proving your situation in Spain.
